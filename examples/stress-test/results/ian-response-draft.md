# Draft response to Ian (convex-backend issue #414)

Posted as a follow-up on the OOM thread. Stays factual, leads with the
fix that worked, ends with three questions that affect tooling authors
beyond just zodvex.

---

Quick update on what we found chasing the OOM in zodvex's codegen path
under the new per-entrypoint analyzer.

**TL;DR: the analyzer works as documented. The surprise was the
entrypoint *set*.** Convex's CLI walker (`entryPoints()` in
`bundler/index.js`) treats every `.{ts,js}` file under `convex/` as a
distinct entrypoint and analyzes each in its own 64 MB isolate.
Tooling like zodvex that emits codegen artifacts under `convex/`
(e.g. `convex/_zodvex/api.js`) has those files picked up too. With
~200 zod-validated functions, our generated `api.js` (a registry
of inline `z.object({...})` schemas) hits 158 KB on disk and exceeds
the 64 MB isolate cap when loaded on its own.

The schema isolate (the one that loads `schema.ts`) was a separate
constraint. We addressed it by emitting a pure-Convex `tables.ts`
that `schema.ts` imports — no zod at schema-eval time. Local
measurements confirmed schema heap dropping from 38 MB to 3 MB at
N=200. But the deploy still OOMed at the same N range — just at
"Loading the pushed modules" instead of "Hit an error while
evaluating your schema."

**Resolution**: drop a `convex.config.ts` (with a no-op
`defineApp()`) inside `convex/_zodvex/`. The walker's
`looksLikeNestedComponent` predicate matches, the directory is
skipped during entrypoint discovery, and the codegen artifacts are
no longer each analyzed in their own 64 MB isolate. With that one
file plus our schema-only-thin codegen, deploys now scale to N=2000
zod-validated endpoints — matching the pure-convex baseline we
measured for plain function modules.

Three things this surfaces that I'd love your take on:

### 1. Is the multi-dot / nested-component skip behavior stable to depend on?

The walker also skips files with multiple dots in the basename
(`*.foo.ts`) and dotfiles, plus nested-component directories. We're
relying on the third — tooling consumers will rely on at least one of
the first two if they ship codegen artifacts inside `convex/`.

Confirming these are intentional and stable would help.

### 2. The per-entrypoint analyzer is great, but the entrypoint set is the leverage point.

For tooling authors who emit codegen into `convex/`, a single heavy
file becomes a hard 64 MB-per-file ceiling, even if no consumer
statically imports it. Our `api.js` was the worst case — a registry
that grows linearly with endpoint count and is loaded by Convex's
analyzer purely because it's a file in `convex/`.

Documenting "if you emit modules into `convex/`, here are the
file-name conventions Convex's walker skips" would be very useful.
Right now we found this by reading bundler source.

### 3. A documented "tooling output" convention?

What we *want* to communicate is: "this directory is generated by
tooling, don't analyze its files as Convex function modules, but they
will be referenced by other Convex files via normal imports."

We accomplished that with `convex.config.ts` inside `_zodvex/`, but
we're slightly abusing that file's semantics — it normally means "this
is a Convex component definition." A purpose-built signal (e.g. a
`.convex-skip` marker, or a documented `_zodvex/`-style convention)
would be cleaner.
